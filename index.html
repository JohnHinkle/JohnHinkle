Research

I am a member of the Teske Lab in the Department of Earth, Marine and Environmental Sciences where I study the microbiology of hydrothermal vents and the deep subsurface in the Guaymas Basin. My work focuses on constructing phylogenetic trees for various groups of archaea and bacteria. This work allows for insights into the diversity and biogeography of microbial groups of interest (ex. methanotrophic and methanogenic archaea).

This fall I will be writing my senior honors thesis under the supervision of Dr. Andreas Teske.
